BACKGROUND
A. The Parties intend to engage in discussions regarding potential collaboration in healthcare technology solutions, medical device development, and/or patient care services ("Permitted Purpose").
B. During these discussions, each Party may share Confidential Information with the other Party.
C. The Parties desire to protect the confidentiality of such information under the terms of this Agreement.

AGREEMENT
NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual promises and covenants contained herein, the Parties agree as follows:

1. DEFINITION OF CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION
1.1 "Confidential Information" shall mean all information of a confidential nature disclosed by either Party to the other Party, including but not limited to:
   • Patient care protocols and methodologies
   • Clinical trial data and research findings
   • Medical device specifications and designs
   • Software platforms and healthcare IT systems
   • Business plans and financial projections
   • Regulatory compliance strategies
   • Intellectual property and trade secrets
   • Any information marked "Confidential" or "Proprietary"

1.2 Confidential Information includes all HIPAA-protected health information that may be disclosed during discussions.

2. CONFIDENTIALITY OBLIGATIONS
2.1 Each Party shall:
   (a) Maintain the confidentiality of all Confidential Information received from the other Party
   (b) Not disclose Confidential Information to any third party without prior written consent
   (c) Use Confidential Information solely for the Permitted Purpose
   (d) Apply security measures no less stringent than those used for its own confidential information
   (e) Comply with all applicable healthcare privacy laws, including HIPAA

3. PERMITTED DISCLOSURES
3.1 The obligations in Section 2 shall not apply to Confidential Information that:
   (a) Is or becomes publicly available through no fault of the receiving Party
   (b) Was lawfully known by the receiving Party prior to disclosure
   (c) Is independently developed without use of Confidential Information
   (d) Is lawfully obtained from a third party without breach of confidentiality
   (e) Must be disclosed pursuant to law, regulation, or court order (with prompt notice to the disclosing Party)

4. HEALTHCARE-SPECIFIC PROVISIONS
4.1 Both Parties acknowledge that healthcare information is subject to stringent privacy laws.
4.2 Any patient information shared shall be de-identified in accordance with HIPAA requirements.
4.3 Each Party represents that it maintains appropriate administrative, physical, and technical safeguards.

5. TERM AND TERMINATION
5.1 This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and continue for three (3) years.
5.2 Either Party may terminate this Agreement with 60 days written notice.
5.3 Confidentiality obligations shall survive termination for five (5) years.

6. RETURN OF INFORMATION
Upon termination or upon request, each Party shall:
   (a) Return all tangible Confidential Information
   (b) Delete all electronic copies of Confidential Information
   (c) Provide written certification of compliance with this section

7. NO GRANT OF RIGHTS
This Agreement does not grant any license or rights to either Party's intellectual property, except the limited right to use Confidential Information for the Permitted Purpose.

8. REMEDIES
Each Party acknowledges that breach of this Agreement may cause irreparable harm for which monetary damages are inadequate. The non-breaching Party shall be entitled to seek equitable relief, including injunction and specific performance.

9. MISCELLANEOUS
9.1 Governing Law: This Agreement shall be governed by California law.
9.2 Entire Agreement: This Agreement constitutes the entire understanding between the Parties.
9.3 Amendments: Any amendments must be in writing and signed by both Parties.
9.4 Severability: If any provision is unenforceable, the remainder shall continue in effect.
